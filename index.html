Welcome to Ethan's Art Haven.

Unveiling Passion, Creativity, and Art

Hey there, art enthusiasts! I'm thrilled to welcome you to my online sanctuary, where I share my passions, hobbies, and, most importantly, my art.

Whether you're a fellow artist, an art lover, or someone curious about the creative process, you've found the perfect spot. Dive into a world where imagination knows no bounds, and let my artworks tell stories that resonate with the heart. Feel free to explore, enjoy the vibrant colors, and if you're interested in bringing a piece of this creativity into your life, I'm open for commissions.

Let's embark on this artistic journey together – it's going to be a colorful ride!

featured projects

"Rainy Day Reverie"

Caleb the tiger joins a CICT student while waiting for the bus on a rainy day. With an umbrella in hand, he waits while looking on Caleb's face perplexedly.

I aimed to evoke a sense of calmliness reminiscent of a beloved scene from "My Neighbor Totoro".

"First Step"

Poignant moment of a WVSU freshman's entrance.

I aimed to indirectly portray a sense of nervousness and excitement, the emotional spectrum of a student taking that crucial initial stride into their academic adventure.

"Sunlit Bleacher Memories"

The golden days of high school as warm sunlight bathes the bleachers. Amid the lively atmosphere of a school event rehearsal, a student finds a moment of respite by indulging in the simple joy of popcorn from the school canteen.

Laughters linger in the air — a tableau of carefree bliss under the radiant sunshine. I aim to make us remember and relive those golden days, where things where a bit simpler.

"Soda Sip Under the Sun "

Student's sanctuary, a wooden platform nestled against a tiangge. With a soda in hand, the student leans back, gazing contemplatively towards the sunlit sky.

The gentle breeze plays a lullaby that almost coaxing him into a slumber. This scene captures a sacred moment of undisturbed introspection—a safe haven where thoughts can unfold without interruption.

I aim to remind everyone that we deserve to rest and think for a bit.
